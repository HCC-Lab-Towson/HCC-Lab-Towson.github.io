What is Human-Centered Computing?

Human-Centered Computing (HCC) is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on designing, developing, and evaluating technology that prioritizes human needs, experiences, capabilities, values, and social contexts.

Why does it matter?

  • Enhances user experience, users' skill and creativity.
  • Ensures ethical and responsible technology development.
  • Bridges the gap between humans and complex computing systems.
  • Drives innovation by integrating human needs into technology design.


Frequently Asked Questions

What is Human-Centered Computing (HCC)?

Human-Centered Computing is an interdisciplinary field focused on the design, development, and evaluation of technology that prioritizes human needs, experiences, and well-being. It aims to create intuitive, effective, and ethical computing systems.

How can I join the HCC Lab at Towson University?

Prospective students (undergraduate and graduate) interested in research opportunities can reach out to Dr. Jeba Rezwana, the HCC Lab Director, via email. Please include your resume, academic interests, and why you are interested in HCC research.

What kind of projects does the HCC Lab work on?

Our lab engages in diverse projects, including human-AI co-creation, ethical AI design, accessibility in generative AI, and human-AI interaction in educational contexts. You can find more details on our 'Projects' page.

Are there opportunities for undergraduate students?

Yes, we encourage passionate undergraduate students to get involved! Opportunities may include assisting with ongoing research, data collection, literature reviews, and participating in lab meetings. Please contact the lab director for availability.

Where is the HCC Lab located?

The HCC Lab is part of Towson University. Specific lab room numbers and building details can be provided upon direct inquiry.

ProjectsOur research projects

Improving User Experience with FAICO: Towards a Framework for AI Communication in Human-AI Co-Creativity

This project explores how to design effective AI communication strategies for co-creative contexts to make AI a more transparent, collaborative, and trustworthy partner. The goal is to empower users with greater understanding and control, enhancing both the quality of creative output and the user experience.

See more

MOSAAIC: Managing Optimization towards Shared Autonomy, Authority, and Initiative in Co-creation

This project addresses the urgent need to rethink how control is negotiated and distributed in human-AI interactions. It explores how AI systems can adjust their level of autonomy, take or yield initiative appropriately, and manage decision authority in ways that align with user intent and task context.

See more

Prompting AI in Co-Creation: The Role of Syntax and Sentiment in Shaping AI-Generated Content

This project investigates how users craft prompts when collaborating with GenAI systems in creative tasks and how prompts impact co-creativity. The ultimate goal is to develop a comprehensive framework that guides effective prompt writing, improves user-AI interaction, and enhances both creativity and learning.

See more

OrangeBoard: An Empathetic Educational Chatbot Built with Temperature

This project addresses the gap in emotionally intelligent educational chatbots by developing context-aware “edubots” that use conversational cues like tone and humor. It aims to rigorously evaluate the impact of these systems on student engagement, trust, and learning outcomes.

The Accessibility Landscape of Co-Creative AI Systems: Analysis, Insights and Recommendations

This project investigates the accessibility of generative AI systems for users with disabilities, identifies accessibility violations, and offers design recommendations for more inclusive tools.

Paper Accepted to ICCC 2025

July 2025

Our paper, 'Prompting AI in Co-Creation: The Role of Syntax and Sentiment in Shaping AI-Generated Content,' was accepted to the 16th International Conference on Computational Creativity (ICCC 2025) and presented in person by Giancarlo Colloca, a Ph.D. student in the HCC Lab, in Campinas, Brazil. In this study, we analyzed how users’ prompting styles—particularly their syntax and sentiment—affect ChatGPT’s storytelling behavior in co-creative contexts.

Paper Accepted to IUI Workshop on Human-AI Co-Creation

April 2025

Our paper, 'The Accessibility Landscape of Co-Creative AI Systems: Analysis, Insights, and Recommendations,' has been accepted to the 6th IUI Workshop on Human-AI Co-Creation with Generative Models 2025. In this paper, we analyze the accessibility features of 26 co-creative AI systems, uncovering common accessibility challenges and offering strategies for developing more inclusive and humane AI systems for co-creation. Makenzie Preston, an undergraduate researcher of the HCC lab at the time, proudly represented the lab by presenting the paper at the conference held in Cagliari, Italy.
